Lesson 1: Introduction to Social and Professional Issues

Computer Ethics

Computer ethics is the set of commonly agreed principles that govern the use of computers. It consists of philosophical guidelines or moral standards that aim to influence behavior and prevent harm, based on well-founded standards of right and wrong.

The Ten Commandments of Computer Ethics

1. Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people
2. Thou shalt not interfere with other people's computer work
3. Thou shalt not snoop around in other people's files
4. Thou shalt not use a computer to steal
5. Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness
6. Thou shalt not use or copy software for which you have not paid
7. Thou shalt not use other people's computer resources without authorization
8. Thou shalt not appropriate other people's intellectual output
9. Thou shalt think about the social consequences of the program you write
10. Thou shalt use a computer in ways that show consideration and respect

Professional Ethics

A profession is a disciplined group of individuals who adhere to ethical standards and possess special knowledge and skills. Professional ethics are standards set by professional organizations for behavior and values within specific fields.

Universal Ethical Principles
Honesty
Trustworthiness
Loyalty
Respect for others
Adherence to law
Doing good
Accountability

Professional Responsibilities

Legal and moral duty of professionals to apply knowledge in ways that benefit clients and society without causing injury. Key responsibilities include mastering work, enhancing communication, acting as leaders, maintaining honesty and confidentiality, embracing positive attitudes, collaborating, possessing ambition, and serving as whistleblowers when necessary.

Lesson 2: Ethical Codes and Philippine Laws

ACM Code of Ethics

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) Code is designed to inspire and guide ethical conduct of all computing professionals. It serves as a basis for remediation when violations occur, with the public good as the primary consideration.

Five Core Ethical Codes
  • Integrity - Straightforward and honest in all relationships
  • Objectivity - Avoid bias and conflicts of interest
  • Professional Competence - Maintain current knowledge and skills
  • Confidentiality - Respect confidential information
  • Professional Behavior - Comply with laws and avoid discrediting the profession

Philippine Cybercrime Laws

RA 10175 - Cybercrime Prevention Act (2012)

Addresses crimes committed against and through computer systems. Covers 16 types of cybercrime including illegal access, data interference, cybersquatting, and cybersex.

RA 8792 - E-Commerce Act (2000)

Provides recognition and use of electronic commercial and non-commercial transactions, with penalties for unlawful use.

RA 10173 - Data Privacy Act (2012)

Protects fundamental human right of privacy while ensuring free flow of information to promote innovation and growth.

Lesson 3: Copyright Issues and Trademarks

Philippine Copyright Law (RA 8293)

The Intellectual Property Code of the Philippines secures and protects original works of owners. It covers intellectual property office, patents, trademarks, service marks, trade names, and copyrights.

Plagiarism

The act of taking another person's writing, conversation, song, or idea and passing it off as your own. This includes information from various media sources.

Ways to Avoid Plagiarism
  • Paraphrase properly
  • Cite sources
  • Use quotation marks
  • Reference all materials
  • Give proper credit

Types of Ownership

Legal right to possession of tangible or intangible property. Includes corporeal/incorporeal, sole/co-ownership, legal/equitable, trust/beneficial, vested/contingent, and absolute/limited ownership.

Copyright Duration

  • Photographs - 25 years
  • Films - 50 years
  • Publications - 70 years

Creative Commons and Software Licensing

Creative Commons provides global copyright-based licenses. Freeware and shareware are offered free but may have restrictions on modification and reverse engineering.

Lesson 4: Internet Safety, Threats, Malware, and Antivirus Tools

Common Internet Threats

  • Phishing – Fake emails or messages pretending to be trusted sources to steal information.
  • Social Engineering – Manipulating people into giving away confidential data.
  • Spyware – Hidden software that tracks your actions without permission.
  • Ransomware – Locks your files and demands payment to release them.
  • Keyloggers – Record what you type to steal passwords or data.
  • Wi-Fi Sniffing – Intercepts unencrypted data on unsecured networks.

⚠️ These threats aim to steal data, harm devices, or manipulate behavior online.

Tips to Stay Safe Online

  • Use Strong Passwords: Combine letters, numbers, and symbols. Don't reuse passwords.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Adds extra protection even if your password is stolen.
  • Update Software Regularly: Fixes known security issues in systems and apps.
  • Think Before You Click: Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown files.
  • Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use WPA3 encryption and change default router passwords.
  • Use Legitimate Antivirus Software: It detects and prevents common malware.
  • Check URLs Carefully: Fake websites often mimic trusted ones with small changes.

Why? Being cautious online protects not just your device, but your identity and finances too.

Types of Malware

Virus

Attaches to files or programs and spreads. Needs user action to activate.

Worm

Self-replicates and spreads without user input. Can slow down networks.

Trojan Horse

Disguises as a normal program but performs harmful actions.

Ransomware

Encrypts your data and asks for payment to unlock it.

Spyware

Silently monitors and records your activities, often for identity theft.

Adware

Displays unwanted ads and may redirect you to harmful sites.

Popular Antivirus Tools

  • Windows Defender: Built-in on Windows, provides solid basic protection.
  • Avast: Free and lightweight, good for everyday users.
  • AVG: Reliable with web protection and scans.
  • Bitdefender: Strong security features and low system impact.
  • Kaspersky: Advanced protection and known for deep scanning.
  • McAfee: Offers full-suite protection and a secure browser.
  • Norton: Popular, all-in-one digital safety solution.

Tip: Always keep your antivirus updated to defend against new threats.
